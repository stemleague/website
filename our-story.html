Our Story

Co-Founders: Christin, Sophia, and Elizabeth Lin

When Sophia's summer programs got cancelled, twin sisters Elizabeth and Christin came up with the idea to teach her coding at home instead. However, they thought of all the students around the world who may be in the same position as her and created the STEM League Developer Program.

STEM League's mission is to "Educate and Inspire every student to Pursue STEM" and bring accessible computer science education to young students through a collaborative and interactive learning platform. 		The 2020 STEM League Developer Program team of 50+ college student mentors taught over 300+ students around the world to develop their first coding projects and create a supportive learning community for students. Our team volunteered hours to create the curriculum for our Data Programming and Web Development Tracks.

The 2020 Developer Program has been recognized by Microsoft, T-Mobile, UW CSE and ECE Departments , Parent Map (sponsored by Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation), and UW Daily and we are very grateful for the support from local companies and institutions.

In Summer 2022, we are looking for sponsors and donations to help keep our program running for all students who want to learn.



STEM League

STEM League started at Newport High School in 2015 as a club that promoted STEM opportunities to students in the Greater Seattle area. The founders, Elizabeth and Christin Lin, and club members helped host STEM outreach events at local libraries that brought over 200+ aspiring, young students and families to learn about STEM through interactive workshops and hands-on activities.

In 2020, the Founders started STEM League Developer Program to help young students and their sister Sophia Lin learn coding during a difficult summer. We are always excited to start new projects to support STEM Education.
